Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 15, 2026 · Last updated: July 15, 2026

In short: Nutrition stores your meals, goals, recipes, and weight history on your iPhone. We do not operate a user account database or sell your data. When you choose AI features, selected content is sent to Google Gemini for analysis. You can export or delete your data from the app at any time.

1. Who we are

This Privacy Policy describes how Cookie Technologies Canada (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects personal information in connection with the Nutrition mobile application for iOS (the “App”) and this website (the “Site”).

Privacy contact: cookietechnologiescanada@gmail.com

Person responsible for the protection of personal information (Quebec): Cookie Technologies Canada — contact address above.

2. Scope

This policy applies to personal information collected through the App and the Site. It does not apply to third-party websites, apps, or services you may access through links we provide.

By downloading, installing, or using the App, or by using the Site, you acknowledge that you have read this Privacy Policy. Where required by applicable law, we will obtain your consent before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information.

3. Applicable law

We comply with applicable Canadian privacy laws, including:

  • the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), where it applies;
  • Quebec’s Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector, as modernized by Law 25, if you are in Quebec;
  • other provincial privacy laws that may apply depending on your location.

4. Information we collect

4.1 Information stored on your device

The App is designed to keep your nutrition journal on your iPhone. Depending on how you use the App, locally stored information may include:

  • meal entries, food descriptions, portions, and timestamps;
  • calories and nutrient values (including protein, carbohydrates, fat, fiber, sugar, saturated fat, and sodium);
  • nutrition goals and preferences;
  • saved recipes and quick-log items;
  • body weight history;
  • coach conversation history and attachments you choose to provide;
  • photos you capture or select for meal logging;
  • app settings and usage preferences.

This information is stored on your device using Apple’s on-device storage frameworks. We do not receive or store this journal data on our own servers.

4.2 Information sent for AI processing

When you actively use AI-powered features — such as photo meal analysis, text-based meal logging, or the personal coach — the App sends only the content needed to perform that request. This may include:

  • food photos you submit;
  • text descriptions of meals or questions you enter;
  • relevant context from your journal that the feature requires to answer your request;
  • attachments you choose to share with the coach (such as photos or PDFs).

These requests are processed through Google’s Gemini API. Google’s handling of that data is governed by Google’s own terms and privacy policies. We do not use your journal data to train our own models.

4.3 Information collected through the Site

The Site is a static marketing page. We do not use advertising trackers, analytics cookies, or account registration on the Site. If you email us, we receive the information you include in your message (such as your email address and message content).

4.4 Information we do not collect

We do not require you to create an account to use the App. We do not knowingly collect government-issued identifiers, payment card numbers (App Store billing is handled by Apple), or precise geolocation for tracking purposes.

5. How we use personal information

We use personal information only for legitimate purposes connected to the App and our relationship with you, including to:

  • provide meal logging, nutrient tracking, goal management, history, export, and coaching features;
  • process AI requests you initiate;
  • maintain, secure, and improve the App;
  • respond to support requests and privacy inquiries;
  • comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

We do not sell or rent your personal information. We do not use your nutrition journal for cross-context behavioural advertising.

6. Legal bases and consent

Where required, we rely on your consent, the performance of our contract with you (providing the App), and our legitimate interests in operating a secure and functional product. You may withdraw consent for optional processing — such as AI features — by not using those features or by disabling permissions like camera access in iOS Settings.

Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal. Some local on-device features may continue to work without AI processing.

7. Device permissions

The App may request access to:

  • Camera — to photograph meals for logging;
  • Photo Library — to select existing meal photos;
  • Internet — to perform AI analysis and coaching when you request it.

You can manage permissions at any time in iOS Settings. Denying camera or photo access limits photo-based logging but does not delete data already stored on your device.

8. Third-party service providers

We use the following categories of third parties:

  • Apple Inc. — App distribution, device operating system, and optional device backup/iCloud services controlled by you through Apple;
  • Google LLC (Gemini API) — AI processing when you trigger supported features;
  • Email provider — to receive support and privacy correspondence you send us.

Service providers may only use personal information to perform services on our behalf or as otherwise permitted by law. A list of categories of third parties and information shared can be provided upon request where required by Quebec law.

9. Cross-border transfers

If you use AI features, personal information contained in your request may be processed in the United States or other countries where Google or its subprocessors operate. Those countries may have different privacy laws than Canada.

When we transfer personal information outside Quebec or Canada, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate protection through contractual measures and provider safeguards, consistent with applicable law.

10. Retention

Journal data remains on your device until you delete it in the App, delete the App, or erase your device. If you use Apple iCloud device backup, your data may be included in backups managed by Apple under your Apple ID.

Support emails are retained only as long as needed to respond and maintain reasonable business records, then deleted or anonymized unless a longer period is required by law.

Data sent to Google for AI processing is handled according to Google’s retention practices for API customers. We do not maintain a separate server copy of your full journal.

11. Your rights

Depending on your province or territory, you may have the right to:

  • request access to personal information we hold about you;
  • request correction of inaccurate information;
  • request deletion of information under our control;
  • withdraw consent, subject to legal or contractual restrictions;
  • receive information about third parties to whom personal information has been disclosed;
  • request portability of certain information where technically feasible;
  • challenge our compliance with applicable privacy law.

Because your journal is stored locally, you can access, export, and delete most of your data directly in the App through History and Settings → Export. For requests relating to information under our control, contact us at cookietechnologiescanada@gmail.com. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law (generally within 30 days).

12. Quebec-specific rights

If you are in Quebec, you may also:

  • request information about our privacy practices and the technology used;
  • request cessation of dissemination of your personal information in certain circumstances;
  • file a complaint with the Commission d’accès à l’information du Québec (CAI).

A French version of this policy may be made available upon request, in accordance with Quebec language requirements for consumer-facing documents.

13. Complaints

We encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concern. You may also contact:

  • Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada — priv.gc.ca
  • Commission d’accès à l’information du Québec — cai.gouv.qc.ca

14. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information we handle. No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. You are responsible for securing your device with a passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID, and for maintaining control of your device and Apple ID.

15. Children

The App is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

16. Automated processing and AI limitations

AI-generated nutrition estimates are automated and may be inaccurate. They are provided for informational purposes only and are not a substitute for professional dietary or medical advice. You should review and edit AI results before relying on them. We do not make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you.

17. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised version on this page and update the effective date. If changes are material, we will provide additional notice in the App or on the Site where appropriate. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy, except where further consent is required by law.
